# How to write an app store description that sells

Most app descriptions are a feature list wearing a marketing hat: "Beautifully designed, powerful, and packed with features to boost your productivity." Nobody downloads a feeling. They download a concrete outcome.

## Before

> **A beautifully designed, powerful productivity app packed with features to help you get more done.**

You can't picture "get more done," you can't prove "powerful," and every productivity app in the store says the same thing.

## After

> **Every task you'll do today, on one screen, sorted by what's overdue.**

- **Visualize?** Yes — the one screen, the overdue items on top.
- **Falsify?** Yes — it either does that or it doesn't.
- **Nobody else?** Yes if that's your actual first-screen experience.

## The move

Replace the adjective pile with a fact about what the user literally sees when they open the app. "Beautifully designed" is a claim the user will judge for themselves in one second — so don't spend your headline on it. Spend it on the one concrete thing the app does that they can picture before installing.

**Rule of thumb:** describe the first screen, not the adjectives. Show them what they'll see, and let the design speak for itself.
