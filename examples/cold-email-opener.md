# How to write a cold email opener that gets a reply

The first line decides whether the rest gets read. "I hope this email finds you well" is a delete trigger. So is any opener that could have been sent to a thousand people.

## Before

> **Hi — I came across your company and was really impressed with what you're doing in your space.**

Can't visualize it, can't prove it, and it's the exact opener sitting in every inbox. Pure wallpaper.

## After

> **You reply to support tickets in about 4 minutes. Your three closest competitors take 40+.**

- **Visualize?** Yes — you see the clock.
- **Falsify?** Yes — it's a specific, checkable number.
- **Nobody else?** Yes — it's about *them*, and you clearly did the work.

## The move

Point, don't talk. Instead of *saying* you're impressed, Redpen points at a fact the recipient can verify — one that took research, which is exactly why it earns attention. A number the sender obviously dug up beats any adjective about how great the company is.

**Rule of thumb:** open with a fact only you bothered to find. Effort is the message; the fact just carries it.
