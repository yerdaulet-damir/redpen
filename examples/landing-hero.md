# How to write a landing page hero that converts

The hero is the one line a visitor reads before deciding to stay or leave. It has about two seconds. Adjectives waste them.

## Before

> **The modern, intuitive way to manage your finances and grow your wealth.**

Fails all three: you can't picture "manage your finances," you can't prove "modern," and any fintech could sign it. Also note the leak — the word *and*. Two ideas, half the punch each.

## After

> **See every dollar you own on one screen.**

- **Visualize?** Yes — one screen, all your money.
- **Falsify?** Yes — it either shows everything on one screen or it doesn't.
- **Nobody else?** Ownable if that's your actual differentiator.

## The move

Two fixes. First, kill the *and* — do one thing (see your money), not two (manage *and* grow). Second, replace the abstract verb with a concrete image: "one screen." The strength of an idea is inversely proportional to its scope — the narrower and more concrete, the harder it hits.

**Rule of thumb:** cut every hero to a single, picturable claim. If you're tempted to add "and," you've found two heroes fighting for one slot.
