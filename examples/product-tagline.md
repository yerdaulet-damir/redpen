# How to write a product tagline people remember

A tagline is the shortest test of whether you actually know what you're selling. If you can't say it in a line nobody else could sign, you haven't found the product's edge yet.

## Before

> **Meaningful connections, made simple.**

You can't picture "meaningful connections," you can't falsify "made simple," and any dating, networking, or social app could use it word for word.

## After

> **The dating app designed to be deleted.**

- **Visualize?** Yes — you see yourself deleting it because it worked.
- **Falsify?** It's a real, checkable claim about intent.
- **Nobody else?** No competitor wants to say "delete us" — so only this one owns it.

## The move

This is conflict doing the work. A product that *wants to be deleted* is a contradiction, and the contradiction is exactly what makes it stick — it implies the app succeeds when you fall in love and leave. Redpen looks for the unspoken *but* in what you're selling and builds the line around it.

**Rule of thumb:** the best taglines sound slightly wrong for two seconds, then click. If yours is instantly agreeable, it's probably forgettable.
